Our Journey

The story of PM SHRI GSS MUS

From its beginning as a Mission School in 1896 to its recognition as a PM SHRI institution, the school has grown through service, resilience, and community commitment.

Knowledge Discipline Service

Background

A historic institution serving the Car Nicobar community.

The Government Secondary School, Mus, has experienced a significant journey of transition since its establishment. Originally founded as a Mission School in 1896 at Temple Villa, Mus, the school was managed by missionaries and local educators under the guidance of prominent advisors like Sir Richard Temple.

Over time, the institution evolved from a missionary school into a government-recognized center of quality education. Its story reflects cooperation between the church, government, educators, families, and the wider community it continues to serve.

Enduring Values

Milestones

Key stages in the school's growth

The school has moved through several important phases, each shaping its present identity as PM SHRI GSS MUS.

1896

Mission School founded at Temple Villa

The school began as a Mission School at Temple Villa, Mus, managed by missionaries and local educators with guidance from advisors including Sir Richard Temple.

1947-1960

Joint management period

The school came under joint management by the Saint Thomas Pro-Cathedral and the Nicobars Administrator, reflecting cooperation between the church and government.

1960-1963

Transition to government control

In 1960, the original Mission School split into a Government School and a Vernacular School. The Government Primary School continued at Temple Villa until 1962 and relocated to Ans-Pu in 1963.

1972-1983

Academic upgrades

The school progressed from primary level to senior basic school during 1972-1982, then upgraded to middle school and became a full secondary school by 1983.

2004

Earthquake and tsunami setback

The earthquake and tsunami of December 26, 2004, destroyed the school building. Temporary arrangements were made at the Bishop's Residence Compound, and senior students continued education at shifted locations.

2006-2007

Permanent rebuilding and CBSE examination center

The new permanent school building was inaugurated in 2006 with classes in English and Hindi mediums. From 2007 onward, the school resumed its role as a CBSE examination center.

2022

PM SHRI status

The school was honored with PM SHRI status, recognizing it as a model school implementing the National Education Policy 2020.
